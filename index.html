Event Schedule

November 22 - November 28

Muhurtha Kaal Undruthal

Friday, Nov 22

The start of the wedding rituals

Four to five days before the wedding, both the bride and groom’s families conduct the first pooja that officially starts off the wedding rituals. The top of a bamboo cane is decorated with mango leaves. The priest performs puja, and the bride and groom's close relatives apply manjal (turmeric) and kungumam (vermilion) at five different spots on the cane. It is tied up at the entrance of the house and is referred to as "Muhurtha kaal." This notifies those in the neighborhood that a marriage will take place at this house.

Kalyanam Soluthal - Proclamation of Marriage

Saturday, Nov 23

Formal invitation to family elders

Following Muhurtha Kaal Undruthal, the immediate family of the bride and groom formally invite the grandparents, aunts, and uncles to the wedding.

Kumbuduthal - Offering to God and Ancestors

Tuesday, Nov 26

A prayer ceremony for blessings

It is customary to start by praying to God and ancestors before any good event. The family prays that the marriage should go well without any hindrance and that the bride and groom be blessed with the 16 virtues, including Knowledge, Longevity, Dexterity, Youth, and Perseverance.

Kudi Aaki Unuthal - Gathering

Tuesday, Nov 26

The start of family gatherings and feasts

Two days before the wedding, the extended family starts gathering at the wedding house and formal feast festivities kick off.

Mulaipaari Poduthal - Sprouting

Wednesday, Nov 27

Symbolic rituals for growth and prosperity

The groom’s family soaks navadaniyam (nine seeds) to sprout, symbolizing the couple’s growth. They also build a straw stack representing a fort, which the couple steps over after tying the knot.

Thumbu Pidithal

Wednesday, Nov 27

Ceremony invoking divine presence

The day before the wedding, two married women draw a temple pattern on the wall using thread and rice batter.

Kaluthiru Vankuthal and Korthal

Wednesday, Nov 27

Preparation of the traditional wedding jewelry

The groom’s family collects the Kaluthiru (a unique gold necklace) from the bride’s family and prepares it for the wedding ceremony.

Puram Kalithal

Thursday, Nov 28, Early morning

Protection ritual for the bride

In the early morning of the wedding, the bride’s paternal aunt places neem leaves on her to ward off evil spirits.

Thirupootuthal - Tying of the Knot

Thursday, Nov 28, 10 AM

The main wedding ceremony

The bride and groom are brought to the manai, where the groom ties the Kaluthiru around the bride’s neck with three knots. This ceremony is the official wedding, symbolizing the union of the couple and blessings for prosperity and happiness.

Isaipidimanam - Formal Marriage Agreement

Thursday, Nov 28, 11:30 AM

Official registration of the marriage

After the Thirupootuthal, the fathers of the bride and groom register the wedding in the temple book, a traditional Chettiar practice.

Mamiyar and Nathanar Thiruputum Sadangu

Thursday, Nov 28, 11:30 AM

Rituals welcoming the bride into the groom’s family

In this ritual, the groom’s mother and sister welcome the bride by blessing her with symbolic items like silver Lord Ganesha, turmeric, and rice.
